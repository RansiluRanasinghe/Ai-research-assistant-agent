Project Nebula: Next-Generation Autonomous Drones

Introduction:
Project Nebula is an aerospace initiative launched in 2024 by Titan Robotics. The primary goal is to develop autonomous drones capable of navigating dense urban environments without the use of GPS signals. Instead, these drones utilize a proprietary system called "Lidar-Sync" for spatial mapping.

Technical Specifications:
Each Nebula drone is equipped with a hexa-rotor propulsion system, allowing for a maximum flight time of 45 minutes on a single charge. They are powered by solid-state lithium-sulfur batteries, which provide a 30% higher energy density compared to traditional Li-ion cells. The maximum payload capacity is 2.5 kilograms.

The Lidar-Sync System:
The core technology, Lidar-Sync, uses high-frequency laser pulses to create a 3D point cloud of the surroundings. It processes data locally using an onboard NVIDIA Orin Nano module. This allows the drone to detect obstacles as small as a power line from a distance of 15 meters while traveling at speeds up to 20 km/h.

Safety and Regulations:
Titan Robotics has implemented a "Geofence-Lock" protocol. This software ensures that drones automatically land if they cross into restricted airspace, such as airports or government buildings. As of March 2026, the project has successfully completed over 1,200 test flights in the city of Neo-Tokyo.

Future Outlook:
By 2027, the company plans to release an API for third-party developers to build custom delivery and inspection apps for the Nebula platform.
